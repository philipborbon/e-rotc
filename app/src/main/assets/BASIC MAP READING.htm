BASIC MAP READING

What is a Map? – A map is a graphic representation of earth’s surface or part of it, drawn or formed in scale on a plane as seen from above.

MAP SCALE

 

a.    Small scale- map scales of 1:600,000 or smaller are used for general planning and for strategic studies at the high echelon. The standard small scale is 1:1,000,000

b.    Medium scale- maps at scales larger than 1:600,000 but smaller than 1:75,000 are used for planning, operations, including the movement and concentration of troops and supplies.

c.    Large Scale- maps at scales of 1:75,000 and larger are used to meet the tactical, administrative needs of field units.

TYPES OF MAPS

 

1.    Planimetric- shows only horizontal (flat position of features);

2.    Topographic- Two dimensional which present the horizontal (flat) and vertical (relief) positions of the features represented;

 

 

3.    Plastic Relief- A topographic map printed on plastic and molded into three- dimensional form.

4.    Photomap- A reproduction of photograph or photomosaic upon which grid lines, marginal data, places name, boundaries are indicated.

5.    Photomosaic- An assembly of aerial photograph to form a composite picture

6.    Special Maps- for special purposes such as trafficability maps, modified to accommodate information not covered on a standard map such as subjects as drainage characteristics, vegetation, water supply, cost and landing, beaches, airfield and etc.

7.    Terrain Model- A three- dimensional representation of an area, molded in plastic, rubber, or other materials, it shows some cultural terrain features realistically instead of symbolically.

8.    Joint Operations Graphic- A series of 1:250,000 scale military maps designed for joint ground and air operations.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

MARGINAL INFORMATION -The outer edges of the map contain information which is used to interpret the map thus, users are to examine it first.

Sheet Name- Usually found at the center in the upper margin and right side of the lower margin.

Sheet Number- found in the right upper margin serves as reference number based on arbitrary coordinate system and directional location.

Series Name and Scale- found at the upper left margin, group of similar series map on the same scale, same sheet lines format, designed to cover a particular geographical area

Edition Number- It represent the age of the map

Bar Scales- found at the center of the lower margin, rules used for the determination of ground distance.

Credit Note- The producers and reference method of compilation, revision, old and new identity.

Index to Adjoining Sheet- Identifies the map sheets surrounding the map guided by index

Index to Boundaries- boundaries diagram which occur within the map area as country lines state and boundaries.

Grid Note- Information pertaining to the grid system used, the interval of grid lines and the number of digits omitted from the grid values.

Grid Reference Box – Identifying the grid zone and 100,000-meter square in which the area is represented  

Vertical Datum Note- It designates the basis for all vertical control stations and elevation appearing on the map.

Legend – It illustrate and identifies the symbols used to depict the more prominent features in the map

Declination Diagram – indicates the angular relationships of the true north, grid north, and magnetic north.

Contour Interval- It state the vertical distance between adjacent contour lines on the map.

 

TOPOGRAPHIC MAP SYMBOLS AND COLORS

a.    BLACK – The majority of cultural or manmade feature.

b.    BLUE – Water features such lakes, rivers, and swamps

c.    GREEN – Vegetation such as woods, orchards, and vineyard

d.    BROWN – All relief features such as contours

e.    RED – Main Roads, built-up areas, and special features.

 

Military Symbols in the map- are some method of showing the identity, strength, location, movement of its troops, activities and installation.

Unit and Installation symbols:

1.    A Unit – Rectangle

2.    Command Post – A Flag

3.    Observation Post – Triangle

4.    Logical Installation or activity- Circle
