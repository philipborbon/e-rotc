Military Correspondence

Definition of terms:

Military Correspondence- written communication whereby the AFP Transact most of its business. It concerns every subject and takes many forms.

Correspondence a written communication having two folds purposes (to convey information/to build goodwill).

Non Military (Personal Type) used for correspondence with the Sec. of National Defense, other government departments, civilian agencies and civilians.

Endorsement is a reply of forwarding statement added to Military letter.

Personal Action Form - is intended for use to initiate personal actions.

Routing Slip- is an informal communication sent through wire services to facilitate one to another within headquarters.

Radio Message is an informal communication sent through wire services to facilitate action on a certain matter.

 

A. TYPES OF MILITARY CORRESPONDENCE

Military (Subject to letter)-is used for correspondence with army, naval, and air personnel, as well as with the General Headquarters of the Armed Forces of the Philippines. The letter follows fundamental, style, varying only in details.

Three (3) main elements

Heading, Body and Close (Complementary Ending)

-          Approved

-          Note and Return

-          For Compliance

-          For appropriate action and return

-          Disapproval

-          Concerned

-          No record

a. HEADING this part consists of all the materials above the first line in the body.

1.        LETTERHEAD - top of the letterhead is normally places inch or in the 5th line below the top edge of the paper.

2.        FILE REFERENCES identifying file references is placed at the left margin, usually two (2) spaces below the letterhead and in line with the date.

3.        DATE is placed at the right side of the page, second, line below the letterhead. Usual order is the day, the mouth and the year will the day and the year in numerical, while the month is spelled out.

4.        SUBJECT the subject of military letter should be stated briefly and directly, not exceeding 10 words.

5.        ATTENTION ADDRESS to speed routing, correspondence may be addressed to the attention of an individual of the head of subdivision of a command agency, or office, either by reference to his name or by the use of an office symbol

b. BODY the body of military letter is those part that placed between the heading and the close.

LINE SPACING single line spacing with double line spacing between paragraph is used except when the communication is very short.

PARAGRAPHING when letter consist of only 1 paragraph, the paragraph need not to numbered, although its subparagraph is intended 5 spaces. The second and succeeding lines will begin at left margin.

1.      DIVIDING PARAGRAPH a paragraph of three or fewer lines will not be divided between pages. At least 2 lines of a divided paragraph appear on each page. The paragraph /subparagraph consisting of only 1 line may be placed on the last page together with the complementary ending.

 

MARGINS:

1.      TOP first page (without printed letterhead) typed letterhead, inch or five (5) roller spaces from the edge of the paper.

2.      TOP second and succeeding 1 inch or 7 roller spaces from the top edge of the paper.

3.      LEFT 1 inch or 15 bar spaces

4.      RIGHT of an inch or 7 bar spaces

5.      BOTTOM 1 inch or 7 roller spaces

B. TYPES OF NON-MILITARY (PERSONAL TYPES) LETTERS

1. STYLE it follows the same general style of civilian business letter in the following aspects. It uses a salutation, which is a part of the heading, and a complementary close, which is a part of the close.

2. BODY paragraphs are not numbered, single spacing is authorized when the letters contain more than 9 lines.

3. CLOSED used in lieu of the command or authority line signature and enclosure are also included.

C.   EDORSEMENT LETTER is generally the same as that Military (subject) letter.

Two (2) Types of Endorsement

a. The stamped and checked is used to forward communication without comment. An individual who transmits the correspondence initial it. It is in sequence. Forwarded and Returned are not used.

b. Initial Endorsement expressions forming the body of the Endorsement letter.
