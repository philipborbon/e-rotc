INTERIOR GUARD DUTY

In order to preserve, protect property and enforce military regulations in all military installations, the interior guard system is installed by the commanders. The system covers organization and function, orders and countersigns, composition and responsibilities of the guards (main special) duties of personnel, methods of mounting the guard uniformly followed by all units. The system includes foremost the security of personnel thus, the need to strictly install the system without let up.

            ELEMENTS OF INTERIOR GUARD DUTY

1.MAIN GUARD – Consist of a systems patrols and fixed posts

2.SPECIAL GUARDS – for special security purposes.

2 Types of Guard Duties

1.EXTERNAL GUARD DUTY – Generally in combat zone or outside permanent military installations such as lookouts, listening post etc.

2.INTERIOR GUARD DUTY – which will be the focused.

Interior Guard System

The Commanding Officer responsible for the whole installation or area assigned, one (1) Field Officer of the Day (FOD); one (1) Officer of the Day (OD); one or more Sergeant of the Guard (SOG); a Relief Commander of each relief; and Sentinels of the Guard. Number of night sentinels are increase compared to the number of sentinels during day time.

Definition of term

CHALLENGE – ‘’halt who is there’’ or a secret challenge in words or sounds.

PASSWORD - a secret word or sound used to answer the challenge.

COUNTERSIGN – a secret challenge and a password issued by higher headquarters to ensure scrutiny of acceptable persons to pass the lines.

PAROLE - a special password used as a check on the countersign.

OFFICER OF THE DAY (OD) – an officer acting directly under the CO. he is responsible for the execution of all orders and other duties on a given day.

COMMANDER OF THE GUARD (COG) – the most senior responsible for the instructions, discipline, performance of the guards.

SERGEANT OF THE GUARD (SOG) – senior non-commission officer.

RELIEF COMMANDERS (RC) – responsible in each subdivision such as: main guard, escort guard or honor guard. In fixed system or a team guards as sentinels, sentry or lookout.

SUPERNUMENARY – extra member of the guard.

GUARD’S POSTS – specified area for which the assigned guard is responsible.

 

 

 

11 GENERAL ORDER

  1. TO TAKE CHARGE OF THIS POST AND ALL GOVERNMENT PROPERTY IN VIEW.
  2. TO WALK MY POST IN MILITARY MANNER KEEPING ALWAYS ON THE ALERT AND OBSERVING EVRYTHING THAT TAKES PLACE WITH SIGHT AND HEARING.
  3. TO REPORT ALL VIOLATION OF ORDERS I AM INSTRUCTED TO ENFORCE.
  4. TO REPEAT ALL CALLS FROM POST MORE DISTANCE FROM THE GUARD HOUSE THAN MY OWN
  5. TO QUIT MY POST WHEN ONLY PROPERLY RELIEVED.
  6. TO RECEIVE, OBEY AND PASS ON THE SENTINNEL WHO RELIEFS ME ALL ORDERS FROM COMMANDING OFFICER, OFFICER OF THE DAY AND NON-COMMISSIONED OFFICER OF THE GUARD ONLY.
  7. TO TALK TO NO ONE EXCEPT IN LINE OF DUTY.
  8. TO GIVE ALARM IN CASE OF FIRE OR DISORDER.
  9. TO CALL THE COMMANDER OF THE RELIEF IN ANY CASE NOT COVERED BY INSTRUCTION.
  10. TO SALUTE ALL OFFICERS AND COLORS IN STANDARD NOT CASE.
  11. TO BE SPECIALY WATCHFUL AT NIGHT DURING THE TIME OF CHALLENGING AND TO CHALLENGE ALL PERSONS ON OR NEAR MY POST AND TO ALLOW NO ONE TO PASS WITHOUT PROPER AUTHORITY.

 

SPECIAL ORDERS – orders given to a particular guard in a particular post, orders carefully tailored for a specific post, time measured, easily off or on and for a short time.

INFORMAL GUARD MOUNTING - This is held in the parade ground of the unit from which the guards are detailed.

FORMAL GUARD MOOUNTING- Requires a band and is held in the parade ground of the unit from which the guards are detailed.

COUNTERSIGNS AND PAROLE WORDS – The CO directs this and ensure that any person in time of war who discloses the countersigns or parole to any person not entitled to received shall be punished with death or such punishment that the court-marital may direct (Military Justice Art.101)
