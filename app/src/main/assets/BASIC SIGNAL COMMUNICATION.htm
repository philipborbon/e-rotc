BASIC SIGNAL COMMUNICATION

Medium by which communication is conveyed from one person or place or another.

PHYSICAL COMMUNICATION MEANS

1.         Messenger -- one of the oldest and most reliable method of transmitting intelligence.     This is the most secured means of communication.  Messenger may be classified as to type of service into special and scheduled messenger service, as to made of transport into motor, foot, or air relative rate of speed of each type as follows:  foot messenger 3 to 5 hour/daytime and 2 to 4 miles/nighttime, motor messenger 25 to 40 miles/hour during the day and 15 to 30 miles per hour at night air messenger 80 miles/hour both during the day and night.

2.         Mail-- another means of physical communication.  This is not controlled by communication.  This is by mail forwarding of messages by an established postal system.

3.         Trained Animal -- one of the most efficient trained animals in the field of   communication.

4.         Telecommunication means -the bulk our communications worn always are carried by telecommunication.

-     the word telecommunication is derived from the Greek combining far, for off or broadly, operating at a distance.  Thus, the word telegraph originally meant simply communication from after and the applied to all method of communication across distance.

5.         Signal communication- Is a method or means of conveying information from one person or place top another except by direct conversation.

 

6.       Agency of Signal Communication- Is a facility which embraces the     personnel and equipment necessary to provide signal communication by any particular means of combination thereof.

 

7          Message - Is a though or idea, express in brief, in plain or secret language and prepare in a form suitable or transmission by any means of communication.

8.         Body - This is the though or idea that the originator wishes to express.

 

  9.      Brevity - The text of a message should be clear accurate and brief.

           

10.       Standing operation procedure - Is a set of instructions covering features of operation which lend themselves to a definite or standardized procedure.

 

TELECOMMUNICATION MEANS

A.  RADIO -- is the principal means of communication in many tactical units.  It is used for command fire and control exclusive of information, administration, communication between aircraft and ground unit.

 

B.  WIRE --    one of the principal means of communication and includes the use of field wire, cable assemblies, wire laying and recovery, sound powered and battery-operated telephone, switchboard, typewriter and associated equipment.     

 

C.  SOUND-- this is a supplementary means of communication that is available to all units.  Sound signal are transmitted by WHISTLES, BUGLES, HORNS, GONGS, KLAXONS, WEAPON and other noise making devices.  They are used chiefly to attract attention, pre-arranged message, and speed alarm.

 

D.  VISUAL-- communication are means available to all units and used for transmitting (a combination of audio) as well as recognition of friendly forces among the medium use for this particular means of communication.

CAPABILITIES OF RADIO

1.         Radio communication facilities can be used more quickly than wire communication.

2.         Once mounted in a vehicle, radio equipment should be ready for used and            does not require reinstallation.

3.         Radio is mobile, it can be used serial, amphibious vehicle and dismounted            unit.

4.         Through the use of relay circuit, it communicates for a distance in case of VHF-HF.

5.         It has different mode of operation such as voice radio telephony, telegraph, teletype, visual transformation and transmission.

LIMITATIONS:

1.         Radio is subject to equipment breakdown.

2.         Radio is subject to interference.

3.         Radio is the least secured means of communication.

TACTICAL APPLICATIONS:

1.         Main unit is moving or operating.  It is necessary to maintain radio silence.

2.         Avoid necessary transmission.

3.         Transmit only when necessary or emergency.

ADVANTAGE AND DISADVANTAGE OF EACH MEANS:

A.        RADIO

            1.  Advantages                   

                        a.  speed of installation

                        b.  flexibility

                        c.  maintain while hoops are mobile

                        d.  integrated with wire

2.  Disadvantages

                        a.  subject to atmosphere condition

                        b.  subject to interference

                                    b.1 Natural interference

                                    b.2 Man-made interference

                        c.  least secured

B.        WIRE

            1.  Advantages

                        a.  provide person-to-person contact

                        b.  capable in break-in operation

                        c.  relatively secured

            2.  Disadvantages

                        a.  require time and effort to install

                        b.  subject to sabotage and destruction

                        c.  stopped by terrain obstacle

C.        VISUAL

            1.  Advantages

                        a.  suitable for short pre-arranged message

                        b.  suitable for short distance

                        c.  does not require sophisticated installation

                        d.  does not use bulky equipment

            2.  Disadvantages

                        a.  vulnerable to interception

                        b.  subject to initiative deception

                        c.  stations depend on the line of sight location

D.        SOUND

            1.  Advantages

                        a.  transmit short pre-arranged message

                        b.  suitable for short distance

                        c.  station do not depend on the line of sight location

            2.  Disadvantages

                        a.  vulnerable to interception

                        b.  can be limited

                        c.  adversely affected by battle noises
